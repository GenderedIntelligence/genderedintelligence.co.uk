Our Work in Schools and Colleges

In this context, ‚Äòyoung people‚Äô means students under 18, with a particular focus on those under 16. This includes our work with general student populations through workshops and assemblies and our work with young trans and gender questioning people through mentoring.

We want all teaching and pastoral staff to have a clear understanding of how we work, and hence gain confidence in what we do.

Shaping everything we do in our work in schools and colleges is our belief that every young person should be respected, supported and enabled to thrive, whoever they are, whoever they become and whatever age they are. Everything we do comes back to this fundamental point.

For example, sessions for key stage one might typically involve talking about identities, families and relationships, whilst sessions for year 6 onwards might, depending on the session / area of the curriculum, involve talking about puberty, bodies and sex.

Our work supports schools to fulfil their duties and responsibilities both towards young trans people, and towards their peers.
